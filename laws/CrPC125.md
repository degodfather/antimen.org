# Gender-Specific Maintenance Provisions

## Overview

Several Indian statutes permit wives to seek maintenance from husbands.

These include:

* Section 144, Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS)
* Hindu Marriage Act, 1955
* Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956
* Special Marriage Act, 1954

## Gender-Specific Nature

Historically, maintenance provisions were designed to protect financially dependent wives.

In practice:

* Husbands are more frequently ordered to pay maintenance.
* Courts evaluate income, earning capacity, and financial circumstances.

## Criticism

1. Men bear a disproportionate financial burden.
2. Interim maintenance can be awarded before a full trial.
3. Litigation may continue for years while payments are ongoing.
4. High-earning spouses are not always treated consistently.
